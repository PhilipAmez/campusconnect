Mission & Vision

PeerLoom's mission is to eliminate disorganization in student study groups, enabling focused collaboration that boosts retention by up to 30% and exam performance through evidence-based peer learning structures.

Our vision: A global network of academic communities where students and faculty access tools that prioritize productivity, yielding measurable improvements in learning outcomes and institutional efficiency.

The Problem

Current study groups on platforms like WhatsApp and Telegram devolve into chaos: scattered messages bury key resources, inactive members dilute discussions, and unstructured chats lead to information overload, reducing study efficiency by 40% according to educational research from the Journal of Educational Psychology.

Key Pain Points:

  • Files and notes lost in endless threads.
  • Off-topic distractions erode focus.
  • No built-in tools for review or clarification, prolonging confusion.

Our Solution

PeerLoom restructures collaboration with course-specific hives that auto-organize content by topic and priority. Integrated AI provides instant support, transforming fragmented chats into streamlined workflows that save students an average of 5 hours weekly on organization alone.

Features & Benefits

PeerLoom delivers targeted tools for academic success, grounded in learning science principles.

PeerPal AI

Summarizes notes, explains complex concepts, and generates practice questions in-chat—reducing comprehension time by 25% without leaving the discussion.

Auto-Organized Chats

Exam Prep Support

AI-curated flashcards and mock quizzes from group notes—improving retention through spaced repetition, backed by cognitive studies.

Secure, Scalable Hives

Private or public groups with role-based access for faculty oversight—differentiating from generic apps by enforcing academic focus and data privacy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PeerLoom free to use?

Yes, PeerLoom is free for all users, with no limits on group creation or core features. Premium analytics for institutions are available upon request.

How can lecturers get involved?

Lecturers can join as moderators to guide discussions, share resources, and track engagement. Invite-only for verified faculty to maintain academic integrity.

How does PeerLoom differ from WhatsApp or Discord?

Unlike general chat apps, PeerLoom enforces course-based structure, embeds AI for academic support, and prioritizes privacy—designed exclusively for evidence-driven learning, not casual messaging.

How is user privacy and data safety ensured?

We comply with GDPR and FERPA standards: end-to-end encryption for chats, no data sales, and user-controlled sharing. Academic content is anonymized for AI processing.

Can PeerLoom be used across multiple campuses?

Absolutely—groups are open to all verified students and faculty worldwide, fostering cross-institutional collaboration without geographic restrictions.

What support is available?

Email support@peerloom.online for assistance; response within 24 hours. In-app help center and community forums provide self-service resources.
